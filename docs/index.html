Statutory Retaliation Framework

Texas Property Code § 92.331

  • Presumes retaliation when adverse action occurs within 6 months of a complaint about conditions affecting health or safety.
  • Here: non-renewal was issued just 12 days after the FHA accommodation notice.

42 U.S.C. § 3617 (FHA)

  • Makes it unlawful to “coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere” with anyone exercising fair housing rights.
  • Retaliatory non-renewal is a classic § 3617 violation.

Adverse Action under Federal Law

Under Burlington Northern & Santa Fe Ry. Co. v. White, retaliation includes any action that would dissuade a reasonable person from asserting their rights. Courts apply this standard to the Fair Housing Act; lease non-renewal following a protected housing complaint qualifies as an adverse action.

Legal Consequence: A retaliatory non-renewal is void and unenforceable. If the non-renewal is void, Ms. Copeland remains a tenant and Plaintiffs’ housing-related obligations continue until lawfully terminated.
Judicial Roadmap — Ministerial Duties

What the Court Is Actually Being Asked To Do

The requested relief does not require policy innovation or broad discretion. It requires the Court to perform three ministerial acts triggered by undisputed documentary evidence and settled law.

  1. Recognize Disability Status. Medical documentation from UT Southwestern physicians establishes ADA/FHA disability (hEDS, POTS, MCAS, toxic-mold complications) that substantially limit major life activities.
  2. Enforce Reasonable Accommodation Duties. Under FHA, TFHA, and Texas Property Code § 301.025, landlords must provide reasonable accommodations when necessary to use and enjoy housing, unless undue burden is proven. Temporary housing during mold remediation is the textbook accommodation here.
  3. Void Retaliatory Non-Renewal & Restore Housing. When a non-renewal is issued 12 days after a protected FHA request, Texas law presumes retaliation. The court’s task is to void the unlawful non-renewal and restore the status quo ante.
No Evidentiary Hearing Needed: All key facts are documentary (licensed mold reports, emails, medical letters, photographs). The Court need only verify what Plaintiffs’ own documents already admit and apply clear statutory commands.
