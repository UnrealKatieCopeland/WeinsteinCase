üí∞ What Is at Stake

This is more than a standard landlord‚Äìtenant dispute. The claims implicate:

  • Fair Housing Act (FHA) disability discrimination and failure to accommodate.
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requirements for reasonable accommodations in court access.
  • Texas Property Code repair-and-remedy and retaliation provisions.
  • Insurance bad faith under Texas Insurance Code ¬ß541.060.
  • Conversion, constructive eviction, and spoliation of evidence.

A comparable federal case, Megan White v. Weinstein (N.D. Tex. 4:25-cv-00312-O), also involves mold at the same property and alleges similar patterns of conduct. Together, these matters suggest that the damages exposure may be in the seven-figure range when civil-rights remedies, treble damages, and fee-shifting are considered.

üéØ What Defendant Is Actually Requesting
