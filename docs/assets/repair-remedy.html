2. Texas Repair & Remedy ‚Äì Elements & Where They Appear

Texas Property Code (Simplified)

  • Serious Condition: A condition that materially affects an ordinary tenant‚Äôs physical health or safety.
  • Notice: Tenant gives proper notice of the condition and a reasonable opportunity to repair.
  • Failure to Repair: Landlord does not make a diligent effort to repair within a reasonable time.
  • Rent: Tenant is not delinquent in rent at the time of notice and filing (subject to any offsets/abatement).
  • Relief: Court may order repairs, reduce/refund rent, award costs, and grant other allowed remedies.

How These Elements Are Met Here

  • Serious Condition: BioTex report declares Unit 2145 unfit for human occupancy; DMC protocol confirms the need for licensed remediation.
  • Notice: Tenant sent detailed written notices on March 5 and June 27 describing mold, water damage, and health decline.
  • Failure to Repair: No valid Certificate of Mold Remediation has been produced; remediation remains incomplete.
  • Rent / Payments: Tenant continued paying for utilities and bore additional costs for displacement and hotels.
  • Relief Sought: Repair & Remedy petition and proposed order specify requested repairs, rent-related relief, and costs.
